Daily Operations

Step-by-step guides for your daily farming activities

Morning Routine

Essential Morning Tasks

  • 4:30 AM - First Milking

    Clean udder with warm water (35-37°C), check for mastitis signs, use teat dip after milking

  • 5:30 AM - Feeding Time

    Fresh fodder first, followed by concentrate mix. Check water temperature (15-20°C)

  • 6:30 AM - Health Check

    Check body temperature (38.5-39.5°C), rumination rate (50-70/min), and activity level

  • 7:30 AM - Cleaning

    Remove wet bedding, clean water troughs, sanitize milking equipment (50ppm chlorine)

Feed Management

Daily Feed Mix

  • Green Fodder Mix

    40kg/day: Napier grass (24kg), Lucerne (16kg). Cut length: 2-3cm for better digestion

  • Dry Feed Mix

    15kg/day: Wheat straw (4.5kg), Paddy straw (4.5kg), Quality hay (6kg)

  • Concentrate Feed

    5kg/day: Maize (30%), Soybean meal (20%), Wheat bran (20%), Minerals (5%), Others (25%)

  • Water Requirements

    80-100L/day: Fresh water 4-6 times daily, extra 10% during lactation

Health Care

Regular Health Checks

  • Common Issues

    Check for mastitis (hard/swollen udder), lameness (hoof condition), bloat (left side swelling)

  • Vaccination Schedule

    FMD (6 months), HS (annually), BQ (annually), Brucellosis (calves only)

  • Pregnancy Care

    Dry period (60 days), special feed mix, regular exercise, separate housing

  • Critical Signs

    Temp >40°C, no rumination, discharge, severe diarrhea need immediate attention

Proven Methods

Time-tested practices for successful dairy farming

Milk Quality Parameters

  • Fat Content: 3.5-4.5% (Jersey: 4.5-5.5%)
  • SNF: Min 8.5% (protein 3.2%, lactose 4.8%)
  • Storage Temp: Raw milk 4°C, Processing 72°C-15sec
  • Bacterial Count: <100,000/ml (Grade A)
  • Somatic Cell: <400,000/ml (healthy udder)
  • pH: 6.6-6.8, Density: 1.027-1.033 g/ml
  • Freezing Point: -0.525°C to -0.565°C
  • No Antibiotics: 72hr withdrawal period

Resource Management

  • Feed Storage: Silage pH 3.8-4.2, moisture 65-70%
  • Water Recycling: Plate cooler saves 50% water
  • Energy: LED lights save 75% electricity
  • Biogas: 1 cow produces 2-3m³ gas/day
  • Compost: C:N ratio 30:1 optimal
  • Solar: 5kW system for 50 cattle farm
  • Rainwater: 1000L storage per 100m² roof

Performance Tracking

  • Milk Yield: Peak yield by day 60-90
  • Feed Efficiency: 1.4-1.7kg milk/kg feed
  • Reproduction: 85% conception rate target
  • Calf Growth: 700-800g daily gain
  • Mortality Rate: <5% adult, <10% calves
  • Labor: 1 worker per 20-25 cows
  • Cost/Liter: Track feed, labor, health
